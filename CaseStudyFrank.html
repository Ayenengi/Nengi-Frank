Case Study

Hope Collaborative

Hope collaborative mission statement is to “advance racial, economic, and health equity in Oakland through community-driven food and neighborhood initiatives” They “envision a vibrant Oakland where historically marginalized communities shape their neighborhoods’ future, have equitable opportunities for healthy food and safe community spaces, and build community wealth.”

Hope Collaborative partners with many local organizations around Oakland like: Alameda County Public Health Department, City of Oakland, Parks and Recreation, Community Alliance with Family Farmers (CAFF), etc.

Hope Collaborative funders are The W. K. Kellogg Foundation,The California Endowment, Alameda County Public Health Department,The City Of Oakland Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Tax, Sunlight Giving
The positive impact of this organization is that they address the growing issue of food insecurity in low income communities and allow the youth that live in these communities to come up with ways and carry out projects that will benefit their community.

They focus on health equality in food systems, in the Oakland neighborhood by playing a critical role in city planning, and community and youth engagement by allowing youth voices to be heard.
Not enough funding to keep the youth program alive. Unexpected staff change that allows the program to be short staffed.

Technology can be used to improve this by calling for more funding through social media or their website.
