Root Causes

Low income communities tend to have a higher rate of health issues due to the lack of accessibility to healthy food, which leads these communities to take matters into their own hands.
  • Lack of resources (i.e. finances or local supermarkets) leads low-income families to eat fast food which leads them to having a higher chance of poor health/health issues.
  • Low income communities/minority communities (like the ones in Philadelphia) coming together to create community gardens where healthy fruits/vegetables are grown and accessible to their communities.
“Black and low-income neighborhoods tend to have higher concentrations of fast-food restaurants and low produce supply stores. Limited access to and consumption of nutrient-rich foods is associated with poor health outcomes.”
Black and low income communities don't have the same access to big chain supermarkets like the middle class or white families. Without this access they have to result in cheap fast food options. Continuously eating these fast foods leads to health issues.
“...Philadelphia neighborhoods with higher populations of Black people and neighborhoods with lower incomes, on average, tend to have more community gardens and urban farms.”
Due to not having the same access to these same stores, the Black communities in Philadelphia have started their own community garden so their residents have access to healthy food/ingredients.
